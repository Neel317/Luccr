An Example

Imagine that an organization like MD Anderson Cancer Center wants to tell people about the risk of sun exposure even when indoors and encourage them to wear sunscreen even when they aren’t going to be outside.

What should they do to be most effective in their communications? There is so much research on risk communication that it can be hard to know! This app can help.

How does it work?

  • Easy and Quick

    A quick “boot-camp” on what we know from the behavioral sciences about how people respond to risk information.

  • Fillout the information

    A questionnaire that will help walk you through gathering the relevant information for your situation.

  • Download & Pitch

    A customized PowerPoint slide deck that you can use to share what you’ve learned, shape conversations with colleagues, and use best practices in developing communication materials.

Frequently Asked Questions

This app was designed to be helpful to anybody in public service (i.e., public health, financial literacy, environmental protection) or small business to use cutting-edge knowledge to design effective communication strategies. We are academic professors who are often asked to help large corporations and global groups, but we wanted to create a tool to help the kind of organizations that may not have the budget to hire big communications consultants. Because the tips we share here are based on the best from multidisciplinary research, this tool may also be used by established communication professionals to stay current with best practices.

The best way to begin is to start with the questionnaire. This questionnaire will ask you about what you want to do (e.g., encourage people to wear sunscreen to protect against skin cancer risk) and you can type in your initial thinking. The questionnaire acts as a ‘bootcamp’ to educate you about important issues as you design communications to spread this message. Finishing the questionnaire will create a PowerPoint presentation that you can download. It will put together all your answers and our best practices into a presentation that you can use to guide your group in designing more effective communications.
